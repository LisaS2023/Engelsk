2. Inference and critical reading

Use what you have read to answer these more challenging questions. There may be more than one valid answer.

1. Why is the title "Crush" effective?

It captures both the romantic feeling and the overwhelming, difficult nature of that feeling
It refers to a sports match Charlie loses
It only means someone Charlie dislikes

2. What does Charlie's inability to concentrate tell us?

His feelings for Nick are taking over his thoughts and daily life
He is lazy and does not care about school
He has a medical condition

3. What is the narrative tension in "Crush"?

Whether Charlie and Nick will finish a school project
The gap between Charlie's secret feelings and Nick's unawareness
Whether Nick will move schools

4. How does Alice Oseman use visual metaphors in this chapter?

She draws hearts and floating images around Charlie to externalise his internal feelings
She only uses realistic, factual images
The visuals have no special meaning

5. Why might Nick be written as unaware of Charlie's feelings?

Nick is deliberately cruel
It creates dramatic irony and reader empathy for Charlie
Nick has never met anyone before

6. How does "Crush" build on what we learned in "Meet"?

The friendship established in "Meet" makes Charlie's developing romantic feelings more complex and painful
The two chapters are completely unrelated
Charlie and Nick meet for the first time again

3. Visual and narrative analysis

Visual metaphor analysis

Alice Oseman uses floating hearts, leaves, and other visual elements around Charlie when he is near Nick. What is the effect of this technique? What does it communicate?

Dramatic irony

Dramatic irony is when the reader knows something that a character does not. How does Alice Oseman use dramatic irony in "Crush"? What is the effect on the reader?

Pacing and panel choice

Alice Oseman sometimes uses full-page or large panels for important emotional moments. How does she use pacing in "Crush" to build emotion?

Comparing chapters

How is the visual atmosphere in "Crush" different from "Meet"? What has changed?

4. Themes and identity

Sexuality and identity

Charlie is gay, but the story does not make a big deal of this – it simply shows his experience. Why is this approach important in a story for young readers?

The cost of secrecy

Keeping your feelings secret has a psychological cost. How does Alice Oseman show this in "Crush"? What is the effect on Charlie?

Universal themes

Even though "Crush" is specifically about Charlie's experience, what feelings or themes in this chapter are universal – shared by many people?

Discussion: Is it always better to be honest?

Charlie hides his feelings. Do you think it is always better to be honest about your emotions? Discuss with examples from the story and your own ideas.
