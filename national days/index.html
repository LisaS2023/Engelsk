📖 Read: 17. mai in Norway

17. mai is Norway's national day. On this day, we celebrate the constitution from 1814.

Children walk in parades. They wave Norwegian flags. Many people wear a bunad.

We eat ice cream and hot dogs. Marching bands play music. The royal family waves from the palace balcony.

Everyone says: "Gratulerer med dagen!" — Happy Constitution Day!

Learning Goals

  • I can compare Norwegian and English-speaking national days

🇳🇴

Norway — 17. mai

Date: May 17th

What we celebrate: The Constitution of 1814

Traditions:

  • Children's parades (barnetog)
  • Wearing bunads
  • Ice cream and hot dogs
  • Singing national songs
  • Royal family on the balcony

Special: No military parade — it's a day for children!

🇺🇸

USA — 4th of July

Date: July 4th

What they celebrate: Independence from Britain (1776)

Traditions:

  • Big fireworks displays
  • Barbecues and picnics
  • Parades with music
  • Wearing red, white, and blue
  • Baseball games

Special: Fireworks are the big highlight!

🇬🇧

United Kingdom

Date: No official national day!

What they celebrate instead:

  • 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 St. George's Day (England) — April 23
  • 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 St. Andrew's Day (Scotland) — Nov 30
  • 🏴󠁧󠁢󠁷󠁬󠁳󠁿 St. David's Day (Wales) — March 1
  • 🇮🇪 St. Patrick's Day (N. Ireland) — March 17

Special: The King's Official Birthday is celebrated in June

📖 Read: National Days — More Than Just a Holiday

National days are special days when people celebrate their country. On these days, people feel proud and happy to be part of a community. They often wear traditional clothes, listen to music, watch parades, and eat special food.

In Norway, 17. mai is a day for children. Children walk in parades and wave Norwegian flags. Many people wear a bunad — a traditional Norwegian costume. Families eat hot dogs and ice cream. The mood is happy and friendly.

In the United States, the 4th of July celebrates independence from Britain in 1776. People watch big fireworks displays, have barbecues, and wear red, white, and blue clothes. It is one of the biggest celebrations in the USA.

Not all countries have the same kind of national day. Some countries have military parades. Others focus on music and dancing. Some people celebrate with family at home, others go to big public events.

For many people, national days are a time to think about where they come from and what their country means to them. It is also a time to feel connected to other people.
