A picture book project — where AI is not optional, it's the assignment

📖 The Assignment

You and your group will create an original picture book aimed at Vg1 students. The book must be rooted in one of the Engelsk 2 themes and created entirely using AI tools — for writing, images, and layout.

Think of yourself as a director, not a typist. You decide what the story is about, what tone it has, what the characters look like, and how the pages flow. The AI is your production team — fast and capable, but without your judgment and taste.

What is a picture book?

Each page typically has one illustration and a few sentences. The story should have a clear beginning, middle, and end. Language must be clear and engaging for a Vg1 reader — avoid overly complex vocabulary, but write with style and intention.

The prompt log

Throughout the project you keep a prompt log — save your most important prompts, note what you used the result for, whether you changed it and why. This is submitted with your book. It shows you worked with AI, not around it.

What to log: the prompt · which tool · what it was for · did you change it? · why?
Not every message — just the ones that shaped your book.
No grades, no marks. This project is not formally assessed. The only thing at stake is the Award Ceremony on Friday 29 May — where a jury of AI and two teachers picks the winners in four categories. Make something you are proud of.

📋 What Your Book Must Include

  • A cover page with title, group names, and an AI-generated illustration
  • At least 8 content pages — each with an AI-generated image and written text
  • A back cover
  • A clear story arc: beginning, middle, and end — connected to your theme
  • Language aimed at Vg1 students: clear, engaging, appropriately levelled
  • Consistent visual style — all images should look like the same book
  • An 'AI Credits' page at the end: tools used, what for, and a short reflection
  • Layout in Canva, Book Creator, or equivalent — not Word or Google Docs
  • Submitted as PDF + prompt log by end of Thursday 21 May
Mandatory AI use: Using AI is not just allowed — it is required. Groups that cannot show evidence of AI use in their prompt log will be asked to redo the work. The skill being practised is your ability to direct, refine, and evaluate AI output — not avoid it.

Prompt 1

Choose Your Theme

Each group chooses one theme. Have your own idea? Go for Theme 7 — but get it approved first.

Theme 1
My first day
A young person's first day somewhere new — a school, a country, a city. Small moments, big feelings.
Theme 2
Far from home
Someone who has moved to an English-speaking country. What do they miss, discover, and find surprising?
Theme 3
The thing nobody talks about
A story about mental health — anxiety, loneliness, or feeling like you don't fit in. Told gently and honestly.
Theme 4
A day that changed everything
One ordinary day that turns out to be anything but. A decision, a meeting, a mistake — and how it changes the main character.
Theme 5
Two worlds
A character who lives between two cultures, languages, or lives — at school and at home, in the past and the present.
Theme 6
Dear future me
A young person writes letters to their future self — about hopes, fears, and the world they want to live in.
Theme 7
Your own choice
Have another idea? Propose your own theme — as long as it connects to an Engelsk 2 topic and is approved by the teacher before you start.

📝 The Prompt Log

What to log — not everything, just what matters

For each key prompt, note:
  • The prompt — what did you ask the AI to do?
  • The tool — ChatGPT / Claude / DALL·E / Canva AI / Firefly…
  • What you used it for — text / image / layout / proofreading
  • Did you change it? — yes / no / partly
  • Why? — too complex / wrong tone / image didn't fit the style
The point isn't to document everything — it's to show that you made choices. The log proves you were the director, not just the typist.

The Award Ceremony

Friday 29 May · Jury: an AI + two teachers

The project ends with an award ceremony. All finished books are shared and read, then the jury — an AI model and two teachers — deliberates and announces the winners in four categories. There are no grades, no marks, and no formal comments. Just the chance to be recognised for making something worth reading.
