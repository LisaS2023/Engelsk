A2 — Waystage

Easter traditions — Canva project

Make a Canva project about Easter traditions. Compare what people do in four countries. What is the same? What is different?

Your task:

  • 1
    Open Canva and choose a template
  • 2
    Make a section for each country: Norway · UK · USA · your home country
  • 3
    Write 3–4 traditions for each country
  • 4
    Compare: What is the same? What is different? Use but and and.
  • 5
    Add pictures, use colors, save and share

Language focus — comparatives and opposites (uke 13):

"In Norway, people give cardboard eggs. In the UK, people give chocolate eggs. They are different."

"Norway and the UK are the same — both countries give eggs."

"In the USA, Easter is bigger than in Norway. It is more colorful."

"In my country, Easter is similar to / different from Norway because ..."

Useful words (uke 13):

same · different · bigger · smaller · more · less · respect · kindness · fairness · community · together · people · care · rights

Research help
Traditions — what to write
NO
Norway
Påske
Cardboard eggs
Large cardboard eggs filled with candy — a uniquely Norwegian Easter tradition
Cabin trips — hytteliv
Families ski and relax in mountain cabins during the Easter holiday
Crime novels — påskekrim
Norwegians read crime books and watch crime TV — a beloved national habit
Birch branches — påskeris
Branches decorated with colorful feathers placed in vases at home
Yellow everywhere
Yellow chicks, daffodils, and decorations are the symbol of Norwegian Easter
GB
United Kingdom
Easter
Chocolate eggs
Large foil-wrapped chocolate eggs are given as gifts — a major UK tradition
Hot cross buns
Spiced sweet bread rolls with a cross on top, eaten on Good Friday
Egg rolling
Boiled eggs rolled down a hill in a race — a classic British Easter game
Easter bunny
The Easter bunny hides chocolate eggs for children to find in the garden
Simnel cake
A fruit cake topped with marzipan — a traditional British Easter bake
US
United States
Easter
Egg hunt
Children search for hidden plastic eggs filled with candy outdoors
Easter basket
The Easter bunny leaves a basket filled with candy and small gifts
White House egg roll
A famous annual Easter Monday event on the White House lawn in D.C.
Church services
Many American families attend Easter Sunday church together
Dyeing eggs
Families color hard-boiled eggs with dye kits — a popular Easter activity
