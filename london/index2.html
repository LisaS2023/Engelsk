up to 45 pts
🏅 PERFECT
🌐 Open Street View Intelligence Feed
📡 intel report — read carefully
"Big Ben" is actually the nickname for the Great Bell inside the Elizabeth Tower. The tower is part of the Palace of Westminster, where the UK Parliament meets. The clock has four faces, each 7 metres wide. The tower was completed in 1859 and stands 96 metres tall. After restoration work completed in 2022, the tower is now painted in its original green and gold colours. The bell itself weighs over 13 tonnes.

🌉 Tower Bridge

📍 SE1 2UP · SOUTHWARK
up to 45 pts
🏅 PERFECT
🌐 Open Street View Intelligence Feed
📡 intel report — read carefully
Tower Bridge is one of the world's most recognisable bridges. It was built between 1886 and 1894 and crosses the River Thames near the Tower of London. The bridge is a bascule bridge — its two central sections can lift up to allow tall ships to pass through. This happens about 800 times per year. The bridge is painted in blue and white and has two tall Victorian Gothic towers. There is also a high-level glass walkway where visitors can walk 42 metres above the river.

🎡 The London Eye

📍 SE1 7PB · SOUTH BANK
up to 45 pts
🏅 PERFECT
🌐 Open Street View Intelligence Feed
📡 intel report — read carefully
The London Eye is a giant observation wheel on the South Bank of the River Thames. It was built to mark the year 2000 and opened on 31 December 1999. The wheel is 135 metres tall and has 32 capsules, each holding up to 25 people. One full rotation takes 30 minutes. On a clear day, you can see up to 40 kilometres in every direction. It is one of the most visited tourist attractions in the world, with over 3.5 million visitors per year.

🛍️ Oxford Street

📍 W1C 1JN · WEST END
up to 45 pts
🏅 PERFECT
🌐 Open Street View Intelligence Feed
📡 intel report — read carefully
Oxford Street is Europe's busiest shopping street, with around 500,000 visitors every day. It is 1.9 kilometres long and has over 300 shops. The street is home to famous department stores including Selfridges and John Lewis. Every year at Christmas, the street is decorated with spectacular lights that attract millions of visitors. The street gets its name from the Earl of Oxford, who owned land in the area in the 1700s. In 2026, part of Oxford Street is being pedestrianised — cars will no longer be allowed.

⛪ St Paul's Cathedral

📍 EC4M 8AD · CITY OF LONDON
up to 45 pts + 🏆 BONUS
🏅 PERFECT
🌐 Open Street View Intelligence Feed
📡 intel report — read carefully
St Paul's Cathedral is one of the most iconic buildings in London. Designed by the architect Sir Christopher Wren, it was built between 1675 and 1710 after the original was destroyed in the Great Fire of London in 1666. The dome is 111 metres high and was the tallest building in London for over 250 years. Inside, the famous Whispering Gallery allows you to hear someone whisper from 34 metres away. Famous events here include the funeral of Winston Churchill (1965) and the wedding of Prince Charles and Lady Diana (1981).

📡 intel report — read carefully
The London Eye is a giant observation wheel on the South Bank of the River Thames. It was built to mark the year 2000 and opened on 31 December 1999. The wheel is 135 metres tall and has 32 capsules, each holding up to 25 people. One full rotation takes 30 minutes. On a clear day, you can see up to 40 kilometres in every direction. It is one of the most visited tourist attractions in the world, with over 3.5 million visitors per year.

📡 intel report — read carefully
Oxford Street is Europe's busiest shopping street, with around 500,000 visitors every day. It is 1.9 kilometres long and has over 300 shops. The street is home to famous department stores including Selfridges and John Lewis. Every year at Christmas, the street is decorated with spectacular lights that attract millions of visitors. The street gets its name from the Earl of Oxford, who owned land in the area in the 1700s. In 2026, part of Oxford Street is being pedestrianised — cars will no longer be allowed.

⛪ St Paul's Cathedral

📍 EC4M 8AD · CITY OF LONDON
up to 45 pts + 🏆 BONUS
🏅 PERFECT
🌐 Open Street View Intelligence Feed
📡 intel report — read carefully
St Paul's Cathedral is one of the most iconic buildings in London. Designed by the architect Sir Christopher Wren, it was built between 1675 and 1710 after the original was destroyed in the Great Fire of London in 1666. The dome is 111 metres high and was the tallest building in London for over 250 years. Inside, the famous Whispering Gallery allows you to hear someone whisper from 34 metres away. Famous events here include the funeral of Winston Churchill (1965) and the wedding of Prince Charles and Lady Diana (1981).
