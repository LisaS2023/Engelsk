US Education - Level A1

School in America

American children go to school for 12 years.

Before School

Before school, many children go to pre-school. They play and learn. They learn to read and write. In pre-school, children learn many things. They learn about colors, numbers, and letters.

Starting School

Children start school at age five. A teacher helps the students. The teacher is nice and patient. Students like their teacher.

Learning Subjects

Students learn subjects like English, math, and science. In school, a student learns many things. Students have a teacher in class. The class has students of the same age.

High School

In high school, students can choose some subjects. This is different from elementary school. Students can choose art, music, or sports.

US Education - Level A2

American Schools

American children go to school for 12 years. The first day of school is very important for children and parents.

Before School - Pre-school and Daycare

Before school, many children go to daycare or pre-school. These programs are important for children. They focus on play and interaction with other children. But they also teach the alphabet and numbers.

Kindergarten

At age five, most children start kindergarten. Kindergarten is required in most states.

Elementary School

After kindergarten, children go to elementary school for five or six years.

Middle and High School

After elementary school, students go to middle school or high school. In high school, students can choose many subjects.

US Education - Level B1

Education System in the United States

American children go to school for 12 years. The first day of school is an important event, not only for the child but also for parents and grandparents.

Kindergarten and Elementary School

Before starting school, many children attend daycare or pre-school. These programs mainly focus on play and the development of social skills, but they also introduce children to learning. For example, children may begin to learn the alphabet and simple numbers at an early age. At the age of five, most children start kindergarten, where they spend one year before moving on to first grade. In most states, kindergarten is compulsory. After this, students attend elementary school for five or six years, before they continue to secondary school.

Junior and Senior High School

The secondary level is often divided into junior high school and senior high school. Junior high usually includes grades six or seven to eight or nine, while senior high school includes grades nine or ten to twelve. In high school, students are given special names depending on their grade. A ninth-grade student is called a freshman, a tenth-grade student a sophomore, an eleventh-grade student a junior, and a twelfth-grade student a senior.

Academic and Vocational Courses

Unlike Norwegian schools, public schools in the United States do not follow one national curriculum. This means that schools have more freedom to plan their teaching. Students can choose from a wide range of subjects, and they can often select courses at different levels depending on their ability and interests. There are three main types of courses: academic, vocational, and general. Academic courses are for students who plan to continue to higher education, while vocational courses prepare students for a trade, such as car mechanics or hairdressing. General courses usually combine elements from both academic and vocational subjects.

Core Subjects

Even though students have some freedom, there are still certain subjects that are compulsory for everyone. These are called core subjects and typically include English, mathematics, science, social studies, and physical education. In addition to these subjects, most schools offer elective courses, such as foreign languages, arts, music, and drama. As a result, each student can create a schedule that is partly tailored to their own interests and goals.
